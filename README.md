# Tsunami Evacuation Simulation: Vertical Evacuation Structure (VES) Impact Analysis
## Overview
This interactive simulation demonstrates the critical importance of Vertical Evacuation Structures (VES) in coastal communities facing tsunami threats. This educational tool compares evacuation outcomes with and without VES infrastructure.
Click here to simulate: https://kmashraful.github.io/simtec/tsunami-ves-comparison.html

## Purpose
The simulation aims to:

- Educate community members about tsunami evacuation challenges
- Demonstrate the life-saving impact of VES
- Visualize evacuation dynamics under time-constrained scenarios
- Support planning decisions for coastal emergency infrastructure

## Simulation Features
Two-Scenario Comparison

- Without VES: Residents must evacuate to distant high ground
- With VES: Residents can reach nearby VES structures or high ground

## Key Parameters

- Population: 100 residents scattered across the hazard zone
= Evacuation Window: 15-20 minutes (adjustable: 10-30 minutes)
- Walking Speed: Adjustable from 0.5 m/s to 3.0 m/s to simulate different mobility levels


## Interactive Controls

- Start/Reset Buttons: Control simulation playback
- Tsunami Arrival Time Slider: Adjust warning time (10-30 minutes)
- Walking Speed Slider: Simulate different population mobility (0.5-3.0 m/s)

## Visual Elements
Zones

* Hazard Zone (red-brown): Area at risk from tsunami inundation
* Safe Zone (green): High ground above tsunami reach
* VES Structures: Two multi-story evacuation buildings in the hazard zone

## Educational Insights
Why VES Matters

- Accessibility: VES structures provide nearby safe refuge for residents far from high ground
- Mobility: Critical for elderly, disabled, or mobility-impaired individuals
